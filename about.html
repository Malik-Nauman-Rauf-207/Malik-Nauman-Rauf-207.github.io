About Me

I am an Aerospace Engineer and a Fulbright Scholar currently pursuing my Master of Science in Aerospace Engineering at the Georgia Institute of Technology. My academic journey has been driven by a fascination with the mathematical elegance of flight and the complex control systems that make modern aviation and space exploration possible.

My core expertise includes Flight Dynamics, Control Systems, and Orbital Mechanics. I am particularly interested in the intersection of traditional aerospace engineering and modern robotics (Robophysics), exploring how we can enhance the stability and autonomy of unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs).

Current Focus

  • Masters Research: Specializing in Astrodynamics and Control Theory at the Daniel Guggenheim School of Aerospace Engineering.
  • Professional Goals: Advancing the field of flight mechanics through rigorous mathematical modeling and experimental validation.
  • Mentorship: Actively participating in programs like the GT-Boeing FLT to bridge the gap between academia and industry.
